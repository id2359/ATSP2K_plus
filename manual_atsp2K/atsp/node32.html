  • LEVELS.ref A reference file which contains the strings that represent each state, term and J value in the NIST database and from the atsp2K calculation. This file is used to match the corresponding states between observed energy reported by NIST and MCHF calculation. This file is very important and the success of the program depends on careful preparation. Below is shown an example with the format. Note that "&" is used as a column separator. The first column shows the MCHF representation of the configuration, next is the term. The program combines "configuration" "_" "term" and searches .lsj files to match this string. For example, 
    2s(2).2p(4)3P2  & 3P
    expect the .lsj, .j and .lt files to contain: 
    2s(2).2p(4)3P2_3P
    For the same state the NIST representation is 
    2s2.2p4
    and the term is represented in their energy tables as 
    2P
    . The next column shows the J value as it is represented in MCHF. Then the parity is shown as "e" for even and "o" for odd. Column 5 shows the representation of the state in the NIST format, column 6 shows the term, column 7 the J value. All 5,6,7 are from their tables. The user need to make sure their representation is consistent for all atoms, in some occasions, NIST omits the parent term. In some other cases, the J values are given on the same line, separated by comas, therefore, some editing may be required of the file A.E.NIST. Column 8 and 9 show the TEXrepresentation of state, term and J-value for given level. This is used to prepare TEXtables. 

    % FORMAT

MCHF::CONF        T    J    P   NIST::CONF  T      J      TEX::CONF  TEX::TERM 
     TERM
2s(2).2p(4)3P2  & 3P & 2   & e & 2s2.2p4 &  3P   &  2    & $2p^4$  & \Term 3 P {2}/
2s(2).2p(4)3P2  & 3P & 1   & e & 2s2.2p4 &  3P   &  1    & $2p^4$  & \Term 3 P {1}/
2s(2).2p(4)3P2  & 3P & 0   & e & 2s2.2p4 &  3P   &  0    & $2p^4$  & \Term 3 P {0}/
2s(2).2p(4)1D2  & 1D & 2   & e & 2s2.2p4 &  1D   &  2    & $2p^4$  & \Term 1 D {2}/
2s(2).2p(4)1S0  & 1S & 0   & e & 2s2.2p4 &  1S   &  0    & $2p^4$  & \Term 1 S {0}/

    The second part of LEVELS.ref gives the representation of the LS transitions. Note that the first column shows the groups which are used to compute the LS uncertainties for intercombination lines. The user may control this process by appropriate grouping of the configurations, in this case any intercombination line which is in group UA will use the combined gf normalized uncertainty for all LS transitions which include states from that group (UA). The program tabulates a wide range of data in text format. In addition, some data is saved in tex format:

  • Energy difference = computed - NIST, _A_E_diff.tex
  • Lifetimes, _A_lifetimes.tex
  • Tables of ls transitions, _A_E_diff.tex
  • Tables of uncertainties for energy adjusted atoms, _A_tr.tex.

The output file _A.log contains the following tables:

  • Table LS accuracies. (gf-values on the second line)
  • Table 2: Energy differences (Mchf-NIST)
  • Table 3: MCHF energies
  • Table 4: NIST energies
  • Table 5: % Errors in computed vs. observed energies (Mchf-Nist)
  • Table 6: Lifetimes
  • Table 8: Uncertainties
  • Table 9: Line strength errors

The file _A.lsj.log and sorts all LSJ transitions for all atoms.
